Greenhouse is the market leader in enterprise applicant tracking. It is also one of the most frequently Googled tools by people looking for an alternative to it. That is not a contradiction — it is a product-market fit story. Greenhouse was built for large, structured hiring programs at companies with dedicated talent operations teams. A lot of people buy it without being that company, and then go looking for something else.

This comparison covers both tools honestly. Not to tell you Greenhouse is bad — it is genuinely excellent for the teams it was designed for. But to help you figure out whether you are that team before you sign an annual contract.

## The pricing gap is real — and it compounds

Greenhouse does not publish its prices. Every quote is custom, negotiated between your procurement team and a Greenhouse account executive. What procurement benchmarking data (Vendr, G2 reviewer comments, leaked quotes) consistently shows: Essential tier runs $5,500–$7,000 per year for a 50-person company with 3–5 active roles. Advanced tier — which adds sourcing automation, deeper reporting, and DEI dashboards — lands at $9,000–$15,000 per year. Enterprise (Expert) is custom-quoted and typically starts at $20,000.

HireBest's pricing is public and flat. Basic is $400 per year — a standalone AI screener that runs locally, no cloud required. Advanced is $900 per year and adds multi-user access, custom branding, and bulk uploads of 50+ CVs. Custom Integrated is a $1,500 one-time payment for a full ATS with database storage, role-based dashboards, and API integration. No annual renewal on that last one — ever.

The year-one gap between HireBest Advanced and Greenhouse Essential is approximately $6,100. Over three years, before renewal escalators, that is $18,300.

## What Greenhouse's per-seat model actually costs

Greenhouse's pricing is not per-job. It is per-seat. Every recruiter, every hiring manager, and every interviewer who needs to access a candidate profile or leave a scorecard counts as a seat. The per-seat rate at Essential tier runs roughly $300–$500 per seat per year.

A 50-person company with 2 dedicated recruiters might assume they need 2 seats. In practice, once you add 5 hiring managers and 10 interviewers who need to submit feedback, you are at 17 seats — and the math gets uncomfortable fast. The "starting price" conversation with a Greenhouse AE begins at 5 figures the moment you map your actual org chart to their seat model.

HireBest's Advanced plan includes multi-user access at a single flat rate. Adding a fifth user, a tenth hiring manager, or a new recruiter costs nothing extra. The Custom Integrated tier adds role-based permissions (Admin, Recruiter, Viewer) for teams that need access controls — still no per-seat tax.

## The renewal problem most people miss

Greenhouse was acquired by Permira, a private equity firm. Standard multi-year contracts include annual escalators of 8–15% baked into the renewal terms. A team that negotiated a $6,000 deal in 2022 is renewing at $7,800–$9,000 today — without any change in their usage, headcount, or the features they use.

This is the part that catches people off guard at renewal time. The year-one number is not the real cost. The real cost is year three after two compounding hikes applied to a number that was already larger than expected once seat counts were finalized.

HireBest has no renewal escalators. The yearly plan renews at the same price. The one-time Custom Integrated tier has no renewal at all.

## Feature by feature: what actually differs

On AI scoring: HireBest scores each CV from 0 to 100 against the job description, with written reasoning that cites specific requirements met and gaps identified. Greenhouse has a basic ranking feature and keyword matching — useful, but not the same as per-candidate written analysis.

On speed: HireBest screens 100 CVs in 38 seconds. This is a measured median across batches, not a marketing claim. Greenhouse does not have a bulk AI screening mode.

On setup time: HireBest takes 1–14 days from purchase to live, depending on the tier. Greenhouse implementations typically run 30–90 days, involving onboarding calls, integration configuration, and training sessions.

On integrations: This is where Greenhouse wins decisively. It has 400+ marketplace integrations: Workday, BambooHR, DocuSign, LinkedIn Recruiter, background check vendors, video interview platforms, Zoom, and more. HireBest integrates with LinkedIn and Workday APIs. If your hiring workflow depends on a deep ecosystem of connected tools, Greenhouse's integration library is a genuine differentiator.

On contract flexibility: HireBest has no annual contract requirement. Greenhouse's standard terms are annual with renewal escalators.

## When Greenhouse is the right call

Greenhouse earns its price tag in specific conditions. The clearest: you have a dedicated talent operations function — the talent team is 5+ people, including recruiters, coordinators, and sourcing specialists who need to collaborate inside one system simultaneously. At that scale, 400 integrations becomes a real productivity multiplier.

Structured interview scorecards are the other real differentiator. If your hiring process requires panel interviews with quantitative rubrics, calibration sessions, and documented feedback trails for compliance or DEI reporting, Greenhouse's structured interview kit is purpose-built for that workflow.

The company profile that belongs on Greenhouse: Series B+ tech company, 500+ employees, active compliance documentation requirements, hiring 100+ roles per year, talent team of 5 or more.

## When HireBest is the smarter choice

Most hiring teams are not enterprise talent functions. They are a founder screening 40 CVs on a Tuesday evening, a recruiter at a 50-person agency managing six client mandates, or an HR lead at a 200-person company who needs to shortlist 120 applicants before Friday. For these workflows, a $7,000 annual contract with a 60-day implementation and per-seat pricing is not a solution. It is an obstacle.

HireBest was built for the triage problem specifically. The most painful step in hiring for teams under 200 people is not candidate tracking — it is the 4 hours a recruiter spends reading the wrong resumes before they ever pick up the phone. HireBest compresses that to under a minute and returns a ranked shortlist with written reasoning for each candidate.

The no-contract model changes the risk calculation. If you hire 30 roles this year and 5 next year, you are not locked into a 5-figure commitment that compounds annually. You pay $900, you use it, you renew or you do not.

## The honest trade-off

HireBest is a screening tool, not a full ATS. It does not do offer management, sourcing automation, structured interview scorecards, or the 400-integration ecosystem. If those are genuine requirements — not nice-to-haves, but actual blockers — Greenhouse is the right tool.

If your actual problem is a pile of CVs you need to triage fast, without a sales call, without a procurement cycle, and without paying per seat for every hiring manager who needs to see a shortlist — HireBest solves that problem.

The question is not which tool is better in the abstract. It is which problem you are actually paying to solve.
